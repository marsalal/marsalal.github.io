2022 Highlights

3min
January 7, 2023
personal insight

Here I am, exactly 1 year after my last post about 2021 highlights, wasnt planned like that but something in the back of my head told me to come here and write again. The 2022 year will be in a special place in my heart because of all the good things that happened and all the memories created during all 12 months.

Hey, don’t get me wrong, we had our difficulties to overcome and hard moments, but somehow I feel this year was at some level better than the last one. I was able to settle down and continue making a positive impact in my job and Cata was doing the same on her job. She has been amazing just to handle so many things at once and still doing a great job as a dedicated employee, wife, friend, and daughter; from all the years we have been together for me, this one is the one where she has been shining the most!

Speaking of years as a couple, 2022 marked our 10-year anniversary as married! Time flies when you are with the person you love and working to fulfill your dreams. We decided to go to NYC to celebrate our anniversary and yes, we also went running the NYC Half marathon! What could be better? We were doing all the things we enjoy and love the most: travel, eat and run!

2022 was a year of traveling and after 2 years of waiting and trying we finally did our spring 2020 euro-trip! 2 years later we and our parents (mine and hers) went to Europe and had a blast! Netherlands and Italy were our destinations and we couldn’t enjoy it more! From having a beer in a bote while navigating Amsterdams city-rivers or going to the most beautiful tulip park to eating the most delicious gelato in Florence. We visited a lot of places, castles, and museums which also lead to walking a lot and eating a lot!

2022 was missing the cherry on the top, and that was running, for the 3rd time, the Chicago Marathon in October. Neither of us could have asked for a better day, 3:07 for me and 4:05 for Cata. 2 freaking-unbelivable-hardworked PRs! I was short by 2 minutes to qualify for Boston, but what is delayed, is not denied. I’ll keep working and striving to reach that goal. Thank you, Chicago!

2022 gave us one more thing, but I’ll be writing about it next year, when I re-cap 2023.
